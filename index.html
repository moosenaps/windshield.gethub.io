When to Replace Your Windshield

In New Jersey, as in most places, the rule of thumb is that if the damage is larger than a dollar bill or if the crack or chip is in the driver's line of sight, it's time to replace the windshield. Additionally, multiple cracks or chips, damage at the edge of the windshield, or internal damage are all indicators that a replacement is in order.

The Replacement Process

Windshield replacement is a multi-step process that should always be performed by trained professionals. It involves the following steps:

  1. Removal of the Damaged Windshield: The technician will carefully remove the damaged windshield to avoid damage to the vehicle's paint or bonding surface, which could lead to leaks or corrosion.
  2. Preparation and Cleaning: Once the old windshield is removed, the technician will clean and prepare the frame, removing any old adhesive and checking for any damage that might interfere with the installation.
  3. Applying New Adhesive: A high-quality urethane adhesive is applied to the frame, which will hold the new windshield in place. This adhesive must be compatible with the vehicle's design to ensure a secure fit and maintain the car's structural integrity.
  4. Installing the New Windshield: The new windshield is carefully aligned and placed onto the adhesive. It's essential that this is done precisely to ensure the windshield fits correctly and the adhesive forms a watertight and airtight seal.
  5. Curing Time: After installation, there's a mandatory waiting period, known as the "safe drive-away time," during which the adhesive cures and the windshield bonds to the vehicle. This time can vary depending on the adhesive used, but it's crucial to wait to ensure the safety and effectiveness of the installation.
